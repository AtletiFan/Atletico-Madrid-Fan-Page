Atlético de Madrid: Foundation & Early Years (1903-1939)

Atlético de Madrid was founded on **April 26, 1903**, by a group of Basque students living in Madrid. Initially, the club was created as a youth branch of **Athletic Club Bilbao** and was named **Athletic Club de Madrid**.

Key Events in the Early Years

  • 1904 - The club welcomed non-Basque members and started developing its identity.
  • 1911 - Adopted the iconic **red and white striped** jerseys, inspired by Athletic Bilbao.
  • 1913 - Became an independent club, officially separating from Athletic Bilbao.
  • 1921 - Moved into **Estadio Metropolitano**, their first major stadium.
  • 1928 - Joined La Liga as one of its **founding clubs**.
  • 1930s - Struggled with financial instability and the impact of the Spanish Civil War.

The Impact of the Spanish Civil War (1936-1939)

During the Spanish Civil War, many football clubs, including Atlético Madrid, faced financial difficulties. In **1939**, Atlético merged with **Aviación Nacional**, a team linked to the Spanish Air Force, forming **Athletic Aviación de Madrid**. This was a crucial step in the club’s survival and eventual resurgence.
