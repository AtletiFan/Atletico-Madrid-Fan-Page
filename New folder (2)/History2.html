Athletic Aviación de Madrid (1939–1947)

Following the Spanish Civil War, Atlético Madrid faced financial hardship and was on the verge of dissolution. However, in **1939**, the club merged with **Aviación Nacional**, the Spanish Air Force team, and was renamed **Athletic Aviación de Madrid**.

Key Achievements (1939-1947)

  • 1940 - Won **La Liga** for the first time under manager **Ricardo Zamora**.
  • 1941 - Retained the La Liga title, becoming back-to-back champions.
  • 1947 - Officially renamed **Club Atlético de Madrid**, dropping the "Aviación" name.

Significance of the Name Change

The merger with the Air Force provided financial stability, allowing the club to build a competitive squad. By 1947, the club had established itself as a powerhouse in Spanish football, prompting a final rebranding to Atlético de Madrid.
