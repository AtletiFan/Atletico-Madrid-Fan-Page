The Golden Age of Atlético Madrid (1947–1965)

After dropping the "Aviación" name in **1947**, Atlético Madrid entered one of its most successful periods in history. Under managers like **Helenio Herrera and José Villalonga**, the club became a dominant force in Spanish and European football.

Key Achievements (1947–1965)

  • 1949–1950 - Won **La Liga** under **Helenio Herrera**.
  • 1950–1951 - Retained the **La Liga title**, achieving back-to-back championships.
  • 1956 - Won the **Copa del Generalísimo** (now Copa del Rey).
  • 1961 & 1962 - Won back-to-back **Copa del Rey** titles.
  • 1962 - Won the **UEFA Cup Winners' Cup**, Atlético’s first European trophy.
  • 1965 - Finished La Liga as runners-up, cementing their place as a top Spanish club.

The Rise of European Competition

During this period, Atlético Madrid established itself as one of Europe’s elite clubs. The rivalry with **Real Madrid** intensified as both clubs dominated Spanish football. The **UEFA Cup Winners' Cup victory in 1962** was a landmark moment, proving Atlético’s ability to compete on the continental stage.
