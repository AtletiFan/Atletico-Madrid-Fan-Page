Atlético de Madrid: 2021–2025

Following their 2020–2021 La Liga title, Atlético de Madrid continued to assert their dominance in Spanish football. In the 2021–2022 season, they finished third in La Liga with a record of 21 wins, 8 draws, and 9 losses, accumulating 71 points. Their performance in the UEFA Champions League saw them reach the quarter-finals, where they were eliminated by Manchester City. In the Copa del Rey, they were ousted in the Round of 16 by Real Sociedad.

The 2022–2023 season saw Atlético Madrid securing the third spot in La Liga with 77 points. They advanced to the group stage of the UEFA Champions League but did not progress further.

In the 2023–2024 season, Atlético Madrid finished fourth in La Liga with 76 points. They reached the semi-finals of the Copa del Rey and the quarter-finals of the UEFA Champions League.

As of March 3, 2025, Atlético Madrid leads the La Liga standings with 56 points from 26 matches, comprising 16 wins, 8 draws, and 2 losses. Their home record is impeccable, with 10 wins and 4 draws, while their away form includes 6 wins, 4 draws, and 2 losses. The team's defense has been particularly robust, keeping 13 clean sheets in the league.

In the UEFA Champions League, Atlético Madrid is facing Real Madrid in the Round of 16, with the first leg scheduled for March 4, 2025.

Key player Antoine Griezmann, the club's all-time top scorer, is at a crossroads regarding his future. His contract expires in 2026, but he has been given the freedom to decide whether to continue with Atlético or move to the United States in the upcoming summer. His decision will significantly influence the club's strategic planning.

Looking ahead, Atlético Madrid aims to maintain their competitive edge both domestically and in European competitions. The upcoming matches, especially the Champions League tie against Real Madrid, are pivotal for the club's aspirations this season.
